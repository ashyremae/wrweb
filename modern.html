Modern Education

Systemic racism persists in British schools today. Black children, especially those with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND), are still misrecognised and underserved. Critical Race Theory helps us understand how these patterns are not accidental, but part of institutional design.

Scholars like David Gillborn argue that racism in education is not an occasional glitch but a feature of the system. This is echoed by Heidi Mirza and the Youth Justice Board, who found that young Black students face structural barriers that affect their learning, behaviour, and belonging.
